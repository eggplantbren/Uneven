The unknowns are the sine and cosine amplitudes $A$ and $B$ respectively,
and the frequency $\nu$. The data are the measurements
$\mathbf{y} = \{y_i\}_{i=1}^n$.
The likelihood is
\begin{eqnarray}
p(\mathbf{y} | A, B, \nu)
&=&
\prod_{i=1}^{n} \frac{1}{\sigma_i \sqrt{2\pi}}
\exp\left\{
-\frac{1}{2\sigma_i^2}
\left[y_i - A\sin(2\pi\nu t_i) - B\cos(2\pi\nu t_i)\right]^2
\right\}
\end{eqnarray}
